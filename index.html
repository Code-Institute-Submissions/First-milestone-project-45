Why live in an eco home?

Building in an environmentally friendly way has many benefits, along with being both incredibly rewarding and cost effective too. Not only are you helping to look after the environment but you’ll also enjoy lower bills, increased energy efficiency and your home will require less maintenance over time. With a wealth of information and support available, it is now easier than ever before to create a greener and more energy efficient home. There are many types of eco homes available to buy or self build nowadays, below are examples of three beautiful concepts of eco homes.

Earthships

Built with natural, waste truck tyres and other recycled materials, Earthships create comfortable and cosy living spaces without relying on public utilities. They use the planets natural systems to provide all utilities - using the sun's energy and rain to provide heat, power and water. They are totally self-sufficient The buildings heat and cool themselves, harvest their own water and use plants to treat their sewage. They are energy efficient and conserve rainwater.

Tiny Houses

Tiny Homes are about living simply, beautifully and yet still with everything you need. It’s about freedom from debt and having the economic freedom to live a bigger life, instead of having a bigger house. Living in a Tiny House carries a much smaller environmental footprint, reduces the amount of resources we as humans consume to live, encourages us to consume less in general and are much easier to maintain because the house is significantly smaller.

Shipping Container Houses

To some the thought of building a house out of steel shipping containers fills them with dread. However the real picture is somewhat different as both architects and self build home owners have proved that beautiful structures including houses can be built using this method. The flexibility of adding more space when needed, the cost savings from conventional building methods and the choice of finish makes the shipping container home a thing of beauty.

Related Interests

Off Grid Living

Off grid living means to live on your own without being connected or dependent on urban infrastructures. You produce your own power, use your own water source, and maybe even grow your own food. You can live off the grid and start experiencing a free and independent life. More than that, living off the grid also means you save a lot of money in the long run. The surrounding environment will supply you with all the energy, heat, water and food you need.

More Info
Zero Waste Living

Zero-waste living means aiming to reduce landfill-bound trash to the bare minimum—a worthy goal considering that a plastic bag could take up to 1,000 years to decompose in a landfill. When you look through your trash, you’ll probably find discards that could be recycled, reused or repurposed. Some people manage to fit their year’s worth of waste in a small jar by buying second hand goods, not buying plastic packaged goods. It's not easy and there's a lot of discipline involved but worth it.

Eco Home Products

Eco friendly products are an easy alternative in replacing some of the plastic products you buy. By replacing them, you can virtually eliminate your single use plastic pollution in that area. Many eco products replace the plastic material with sustainable natural materials such as wooden cutlery, bamboo toothbrushes, coconut fibre scrubbers, glass containers, metal lunchboxes, water bottles and tins, shampoo bars and conditioner bars and recycled materials.
